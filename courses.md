# Courses and Curriculum

I teach at the National University of Singapore in areas spanning AI governance, financial
technology, blockchain systems, and emerging technology management.

I created the curricula for **IS4246: Smart Systems and AI Governance** and
**IS4302: Blockchain Innovations** from scratch, with an emphasis on combining technical
understanding, institutional context, and applied decision-making.

## IS4246: Smart Systems and AI Governance

This course trains students to design, deploy, and govern AI-powered smart systems in
enterprise and societal contexts. It combines technical understanding of modern AI
capabilities with applied governance, ethics, and risk management.

Themes include:

- AI system architectures and scaling dynamics
- Human-centered design and AI-enabled operations
- Privacy, data protection, surveillance, bias, fairness, and ethics
- AI governance frameworks, policy toolkits, and safety fundamentals
- Governance simulations and iterative project development

Students develop a governance-oriented artefact such as a policy brief, regulatory
prototype, or decision-support tool grounded in technical feasibility and political realism.

## IS4302: Blockchain Innovations

This course introduces blockchain and distributed ledger technologies from both technical
and institutional perspectives. Students learn how blockchain applications work, how to
build simple Ethereum applications, and how to analyze blockchain systems from economic,
business, and ecosystem perspectives.

Themes include:

- Distributed trust, ledgers, cryptographic primitives, and consensus
- Smart contracts, Solidity, design patterns, upgradability, governance, and oracles
- Tokens, stablecoins, digital real-world assets, and decentralized finance
- Game theory, mechanism design, MEV, market microstructure, and adversarial dynamics
- Public-sector, identity, and supply-chain applications

The course combines hands-on labs, analytical assignments, and a final group project
covering application development, business realism, architecture, and communication.

## IS4228: Information Technology and Financial Services

This course gives students a structured understanding of financial markets and the role of
information technology in reshaping financial services. It connects corporate finance,
market structure, and emerging fintech use cases.

Themes include:

- Corporate finance fundamentals and financial market structure
- The role of information in pricing, liquidity, and market stability
- Payments, remittances, capital markets, risk management, and price discovery
- Algorithmic trading, robo-advisory, blockchain payments, and central bank digital currencies
- Applied case work and group projects combining business ideation, financial analysis,
  prototyping, and communication
